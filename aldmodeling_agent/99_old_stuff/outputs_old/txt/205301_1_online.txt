The conformality of thin ’¼ülms grown by atomic layer deposition (ALD) is studied using all-silicon
test structures with long narrow lateral channels. A diffusion model, developed in this work, is
used for studying the propagation of ALD growth in narrow channels. The diffusion model takes
into account the gas transportation at low pressures, the dynamic Langmuir adsorption model for
the ’¼ülm growth and the effect of channel narrowing due to ’¼ülm growth. The ’¼ülm growth is calcu-
lated by solving the diffusion equation with surface reactions. An ef’¼ücient analytic approximate
solution of the diffusion equation is developed for ’¼ütting the model to the measured thickness pro-
’¼üle. The ’¼ütting gives the equilibrium constant of adsorption and the sticking coef’¼ücient. This model
and GordonŌĆÖs plug ’¼éow model are compared. The simulations predict the experimental measure-
ment results quite well for Al2O3 and TiO2 ALD processes. Published by AIP Publishing.
