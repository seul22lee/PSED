## 1. Variable classification

Below each symbol is assigned to one of five classes:

### 1.1 Controllable process knobs (recipe variables)

Usual ALD‑recipe‑level knobs in/implicit in the model:

- \(T\) – substrate / gas temperature  
- \(p_A\) (specifically \(p_{A0}\) at inlet) – precursor A partial pressure during pulse  
- \(p_B\) – background / carrier / co‑reactant partial pressure (if independently set)  
- \(c\) – effective sticking coefficient of A (in practice tuned indirectly via choice of precursor, ligand chemistry, plasma vs thermal, etc., but not a “knob” in a single recipe; here treated as material/chemistry choice, see below)  
- Pulse‑time \(t_{\text{pulse}}\) for A (not explicitly listed, but implicit in integrating \(d\theta/dt\) and determining \(x_s\))  
- Purge time and co‑reactant pulse conditions (enter only indirectly here; not explicitly in the equations)

For the optimization discussion I will treat:
- Primary **controllable recipe variables**: \(T\), \(p_{A0}\), \(p_B\), \(t_{\text{pulse}}\).

### 1.2 Design / geometry variables

- \(H\) – feature/channel height  
- \(W\) – feature/channel width  
- \(h = \tfrac{1}{2}\frac{HW}{H+W}\) – hydraulic diameter  
- \(x\) – spatial coordinate along the feature length (design: feature depth \(L\), not explicitly given)  
- \(x_s\) – penetration/saturation front location (state-like, but fundamentally tied to geometry; I classify it as a **state variable** below)  
- Cross‑sectional area \(HW\) (appears in \(F\))

So strictly as “design variables” that a process integrator cannot change run‑to‑run, but which define the target structure:

- Design / geometry: \(H\), \(W\), (and implicit feature length \(L\)).  
- Derived geometry: \(h\), cross‑section \(H W\).

### 1.3 Material / precursor / surface properties

These are intrinsic to the chosen film + precursor system (not tunable per run, but tunable by changing chemistry or material stack):

- \(M_A\) – molar mass of precursor A  
- \(M_B\) – molar mass of background gas B (in \(z_A\))  
- \(\rho\) – condensed‑phase density of precursor or film (depending on which expression you use for \(d_A\)/\(q\))  
- \(d_A\) – molecular diameter of A (derived from \(M_A,\rho,\dots\))  
- \(d_B\) – molecular diameter of B (enters \(z_A\))  
- \(\tau\) – factor in collision term (geometry of collisions)  
- \(b_A\) – stoichiometric coefficient of A in the film  
- \(b_{\text{film}}\) – film‑related factor entering \(q\)  
- \(M\) – molar mass of the deposited film’s repeat unit  
- \(C_{\text{sat}}\) – saturation coverage per cycle (embedded in expression for \(q\))  
- \(P_0 d_s\) – effective desorption prefactor (attempt frequency × desorption probability)  
- \(c\) – sticking coefficient of A (chemistry‑dependent; usually considered a kinetic parameter)  
- \(\rho_A\) – gas‑phase mass density of A (appears in the flux expression in your compact form)  
- Film stoichiometry/structure parameters inside \(gP\) in the \(q\) expression

These are “knobs” only if you are willing to **change precursor, coreactant, carrier gas, or film composition**. Within one recipe for a fixed material, they are parameters, not controls.

### 1.4 Fundamental constants

- \(N_0\) – Avogadro’s number  
- \(R\) – universal gas constant  
- \(k_B\) – Boltzmann constant  
- \(\pi\) – mathematical constant

These are fixed.

### 1.5 State / derived variables

Quantities computed by the model from geometry, materials, and process conditions:

- \(v_A\) – mean thermal speed of A  
- \(z_A\) – collision parameter / mean free path‑related term  
- \(D_A\) – molecular diffusion coefficient of A  
- \(D_{Kn}\) – Knudsen diffusion coefficient  
- \(D_{\text{eff}}\) – effective diffusion coefficient  
- \(t\) – time variable  
- \(p_A(x,t)\) – local precursor partial pressure field  
- \(F(x,t)\) – diffusive molar flow of A along the channel  
- \(f_{\text{ads}}\) – adsorption flux  
- \(f_{\text{des}}\) – desorption flux  
- \(g\) – net adsorption flux  
- \(\theta(x,t)\) – surface coverage  
- \(\theta_{\text{eq}}\) – local equilibrium coverage (given \(p_A\))  
- \(q\) – site density per area (film capacity per area)  
- \(Q\) – kinetic prefactor \(=N_0 / \sqrt{2\pi M_A R T}\)  
- \(K\) – Langmuir equilibrium constant  
- \(x_s(t)\) – location of the penetration/saturation front  
- (Growth metrics not explicitly in equations but implied) local growth per cycle GPC\((x)\), step coverage / conformality

## 2. Influence of controllable variables on physics and growth

Here I focus on the **true recipe knobs**: \(T\), \(p_{A0}\), \(p_B\), and exposure time \(t_{\text{pulse}}\). I’ll go through how they propagate through:

- transport coefficients (\(D_A, D_{Kn}, D_{\text{eff}}\)),
- pressure field \(p_A(x,t)\),
- adsorption/desorption rates,
- net reaction rate \(g\),
- coverage \(\theta\) and penetration depth \(x_s\),
- and finally growth metrics (local GPC, conformality).

### 2.1 Temperature \(T\)

#### Effect on transport

- Molecular speed:
  \[
  v_A \propto \sqrt{\frac{T}{M_A}}
  \]
- Molecular diffusion:
  \[
  D_A \propto v_A^2 z_A \sim T\cdot z_A
  \]
  and \(z_A\) also contains \(\sqrt{T}\), so overall \(D_A\) grows with roughly \(T^{3/2}\) if pressures fixed.
- Knudsen diffusion:
  \[
  D_{Kn} = h\left(\frac{8RT}{9\pi M_A}\right)^{1/2} \propto \sqrt{T}
  \]
- Effective diffusion:
  \[
  D_{\text{eff}} = \left(\frac{1}{D_A} + \frac{1}{D_{Kn}}\right)^{-1}
  \]
  increases with \(T\); the limiting regime (molecular vs Knudsen) determines exact scaling.

Net effect: **higher \(T\) → higher \(D_{\text{eff}}\)** → easier penetration, larger \(x_s\) for same chemistry and pulse length.

#### Effect on adsorption/desorption

- Adsorption flux:
  \[
  f_{\text{ads}} = (1-\theta)\frac{c N_0 p_A}{\sqrt{2\pi M_A RT}} = (1-\theta)\,c\,Q\,p_A
  \]
  with \(Q \propto 1/\sqrt{T}\).
  - For fixed \(p_A\), raising \(T\) reduces \(Q\), **slightly decreasing** \(f_{\text{ads}}\) per unit \(p_A\).
- Desorption:
  \[
  f_{\text{des}} = q P_0 d_s
  \]
  has no explicit \(T\) in this simplified form, but in reality \(P_0 d_s\) is Arrhenius. In this model we treat it as constant.

- Equilibrium constant:
  \[
  K = \frac{cQ}{q P_0 d_s} \propto \frac{1}{\sqrt{T}}
  \]
  So increasing \(T\) **reduces \(K\)**, i.e. weaker equilibrium adsorption at fixed \(p_A\).

Trade‑off:

- Higher \(T\): better diffusion but somewhat weaker adsorption equilibrium (smaller \(K\)), and slightly slower adsorption rate per unit pressure.
- The net impact on saturation depth can go **either way**, but in high‑AR ALD, improved diffusion usually dominates, so higher \(T\) often gives deeper penetration but can slightly reduce GPC if adsorption becomes too weak at the deepest locations.

#### Effect on coverage, \(x_s\), conformality

- Larger \(D_{\text{eff}}\) → for a given \(t_{\text{pulse}}\), the pressure profile flattens and gas penetrates deeper → \(x_s\) increases.
- But smaller \(K\) and \(Q\) → need higher \(p_A\) or longer \(t_{\text{pulse}}\) to reach near‑saturation at depth.
- Conformality (coverage ratio bottom/top) is improved if diffusion was limiting; it may worsen if adsorption becomes too weak at the bottom relative to the top.

### 2.2 Precursor inlet pressure \(p_{A0}\)

\(p_{A0}\) is one of the **most powerful knobs**.

#### Effect on pressure field and flux

Under the quasi‑steady approximation:

\[
p_A(x) = p_{A0}\left(1 - \frac{x}{x_s}\right)
\]
and
\[
F \approx H W \frac{D_{\text{eff}}}{k_B T}\frac{p_{A0}}{x_s}
\]

So:

- \(p_{A0}\uparrow \Rightarrow p_A(x)\uparrow\) everywhere \(x<x_s\)
- For a given \(x_s\), flux \(F \propto p_{A0}\)

#### Effect on adsorption, net rate, and coverage

- Adsorption:
  \[
  f_{\text{ads}} \propto (1-\theta)\,c\,Q\,p_A
  \]
  → linearly scales with local \(p_A\).
- Net flux:
  \[
  g = f_{\text{ads}} - f_{\text{des}}
  \]
  → increasing \(p_A\) raises \(g\); once \(f_{\text{ads}}\) beats \(f_{\text{des}}\), \(\theta\) moves rapidly to \(\theta_{\text{eq}}\).

Therefore:

- Higher \(p_{A0}\) → faster local saturation (shorter local saturation time) for any location where gas can reach.
- Also, higher flux can push \(x_s\) deeper before gas is fully consumed in a finite pulse.

Impact on growth:

- Local GPC increases toward its saturation limit as \(p_A\) is raised.
- Conformality improves because deeper regions have enough \(p_A\) and time to reach similar \(\theta\) as the entrance.
- But eventually you hit a **transport limit**: increasing \(p_{A0}\) further only shallowly increases \(x_s\) once diffusion + geometric sink dominate.

### 2.3 Background / carrier pressure \(p_B\)

Enters via the collision term \(z_A\):

\[
z_A = \frac{\pi}{4} (d_A + d_B)^2 
\left[ \frac{8RT}{\pi} \left( \frac{1}{M_A} + \frac{1}{M_B} \right) \right]^{1/2} 
\frac{p_B}{RT} + \dots
\]

Higher \(p_B\):

- Increases collisions with B → can **reduce \(D_A\)** (more collisions, shorter mean free path).
- This shifts the balance toward Knudsen diffusion; if \(D_A\) becomes much smaller than \(D_{Kn}\), then \(D_{\text{eff}}\approx D_A\).

Practically:

- Raising carrier pressure (common in some ALD systems) tends to **lower \(D_{\text{eff}}\)** and reduce penetration depth, all else fixed.
- Conversely, too low \(p_B\) may cause gas flow nonuniformity or instability; there is usually a process‑driven optimum.

### 2.4 Exposure/pulse time \(t_{\text{pulse}}\)

Not written explicitly in the equations, but critical in integrating:

- Coverage dynamics:
  \[
  \theta(x,t_{\text{pulse}}) = \theta_0 + \int_0^{t_{\text{pulse}}} \frac{g(x,t)}{q}\,dt
  \]
- Penetration depth \(x_s(t_{\text{pulse}})\) arises from:
  - Diffusion of A into new regions, and
  - Time available for adsorption to consume precursor along the path.

Qualitative impact:

- Longer \(t_{\text{pulse}}\) → more time for gas to diffuse and react → larger \(x_s\) and higher \(\theta(x)\) at all depths.
- There is a “self‑limited” regime where adding pulse time no longer increases GPC near the entrance but may still improve penetration deeper into the feature.
- Beyond a certain \(t_{\text{pulse}}\), \(x_s\rightarrow L\) and \(\theta(x)\rightarrow \theta_{\text{eq}}\) everywhere: fully saturated and conformal.

## 3. End‑to‑end rate‑calculation chain

Conceptually, from decision variables to growth metrics:

1. **Decision variables (recipe + design)**  
   - \(T, p_{A0}, p_B, t_{\text{pulse}}\) (recipe)  
   - \(H,W,L\) (geometry; given)  
   - Material set: \(M_A, c, q, P_0 d_s, \ldots\) (chosen system)

2. **Transport coefficients**  
   - Compute \(d_A(M_A,\rho,N_0,R,T)\)  
   - Compute \(v_A(T,M_A)\) and \(z_A(p_A,p_B,d_A,d_B,M_A,M_B,\tau,T)\)  
   - \(D_A(v_A,z_A)\) and \(h(H,W)\)  
   - \(D_{Kn}(h,M_A,T)\)  
   - \(D_{\text{eff}}(D_A,D_{Kn})\)

3. **Coupled diffusion–reaction**  
   - Use PDE:
     \[
     \frac{\partial p_A}{\partial t} = D_{\text{eff}}\frac{\partial^2 p_A}{\partial x^2} - g\frac{4RT}{hN_0}
     \]
   - With \(g(x,t) = f_{\text{ads}}(p_A(x,t),\theta(x,t)) - f_{\text{des}}(q)\).

   Under quasi‑steady approximation for \(p_A\):
   - Solve \(D_{\text{eff}} \partial^2p_A/\partial x^2 \approx 0\) with boundary \(p_A(0,t)=p_{A0}\), \(p_A(x_s,t)=0\).
   - Obtain \(p_A(x,t) = p_{A0}(1-x/x_s)\) in region \(x<x_s\).

4. **Local kinetics and coverage evolution**
   - Given \(p_A(x,t)\),
     \[
     f_{\text{ads}}(x,t) = (1-\theta(x,t))cQp_A(x,t)
     \]
   - \(f_{\text{des}}(x,t)=qP_0d_s\)  
   - Net: \(g(x,t) = f_{\text{ads}}-f_{\text{des}}\)
   - Integrate:
     \[
     \frac{d\theta(x,t)}{dt} = \frac{g(x,t)}{q}
     \]
   over \(0<t<t_{\text{pulse}}\).

   The front \(x_s(t)\) is where \(p_A\) effectively drops to zero due to consumption; it is determined by matching integrated consumption to supplied flux.

5. **Growth metrics**
   - Local GPC at depth \(x\):
     \[
     \text{GPC}(x) \propto \theta_{\text{final}}(x)
     \]
     (proportional to \(\theta\) if one monolayer corresponds to \(\theta=1\)).
   - Conformality / step coverage:
     \[
     \text{Conformality} = \frac{\text{GPC at bottom}}{\text{GPC at top}} \approx \frac{\theta_{\text{final}}(x=L)}{\theta_{\text{final}}(x=0)}
     \]
   - Penetration depth:
     \[
     x_s(t_{\text{pulse}}) = \max x \text{ such that } \theta(x,t_{\text{pulse}})\approx \theta_{\text{eq}} \text{ or } p_A(x) > \text{threshold}
     \]

Thus:

\[
(T,p_{A0},p_B,t_{\text{pulse}};\;H,W,L;\text{chemistry})\Rightarrow D_{\text{eff}},p_A(x,t),g(x,t),\theta(x,t)\Rightarrow \text{GPC}(x),x_s,\text{conformality}
\]

## 4. Example optimization problem formulations

Below are representative, well‑posed optimization problems using this model.

### 4.1 Problem A – Maximize conformality at fixed cycle time

**Goal:** For a given feature geometry and total process time budget per cycle, choose precursor conditions to maximize conformality (minimize thickness nonuniformity along depth).

- **Decision variables**:  
  \(\mathbf{u} = (T, p_{A0}, t_{\text{pulse}})\)  
  (Optionally include \(p_B\) if carrier pressure is adjustable.)

- **Given / fixed**:  
  \(H, W, L\); material parameters (\(M_A,c,q,\rho, P_0d_s,\dots\)); total cycle budget \(t_{\text{cycle,max}}\).

- **Objectives** (choose one or combine):
  - Maximize conformality:
    \[
    J_1(\mathbf{u}) = \frac{\theta(L,t_{\text{pulse}})}{\theta(0,t_{\text{pulse}})}
    \]
  - Alternatively, minimize variation:
    \[
    J_1(\mathbf{u}) = -\max_x\left|\theta(x,t_{\text{pulse}}) - \bar{\theta}\right|
    \]
    where \(\bar{\theta}\) is spatial average.

- **Constraints**:
  - Self‑limiting at the entrance (top):
    \[
    \theta(0,t_{\text{pulse}}) \ge (1-\epsilon)\,\theta_{\text{eq}}(p_{A0})
    \]
  - Cycle time:
    \[
    t_{\text{pulse}} + t_{\text{purge}} + t_{\text{co-reactant}} \le t_{\text{cycle,max}}
    \]
  - Process windows:
    \[
    T_{\min}\le T\le T_{\max},\quad p_{A0,\min}\le p_{A0}\le p_{A0,\max}
    \]

- **Use of model**:
  1. For each candidate \((T,p_{A0},t_{\text{pulse}})\):
     - Compute \(D_{\text{eff}}(T,p_B)\).
     - Solve diffusion–reaction to get \(p_A(x,t),\theta(x,t)\).
  2. Evaluate \(\theta(L,t_{\text{pulse}})\), \(\theta(0,t_{\text{pulse}})\), and objective \(J_1\).
  3. Use nonlinear optimization (e.g. gradient‑free or adjoint if PDE solved) to search for best \(\mathbf{u}\).

### 4.2 Problem B – Minimize pulse time for required conformality

**Goal:** Achieve target step coverage \(\eta_{\text{target}}\) with minimal precursor pulse time (throughput maximization).

- **Decision variables**:
  \(\mathbf{u} = (p_{A0},T,t_{\text{pulse}})\).

- **Objective**:
  \[
  \min_{\mathbf{u}} J_2(\mathbf{u}) = t_{\text{pulse}}
  \]

- **Constraints**:
  - Conformality requirement:
    \[
    \frac{\theta(L,t_{\text{pulse}})}{\theta(0,t_{\text{pulse}})} \ge \eta_{\text{target}}
    \]
  - Minimum local coverage at bottom:
    \[
    \theta(L,t_{\text{pulse}}) \ge \theta_{\text{min}}
    \]
  - Process windows on \(T\) and \(p_{A0}\).

- **Model usage**:
  - Same chain as above; \(t_{\text{pulse}}\) is the variable to push as low as possible while satisfying coverage constraints.

### 4.3 Problem C – Maximize deposition rate subject to conformality

**Goal:** Maximize GPC per unit time while keeping conformality above a threshold.

- **Decision variables**:
  \(\mathbf{u} = (T,p_{A0},p_B,t_{\text{pulse}})\).

- **Objective**:
  Define spatially averaged GPC:
  \[
  \text{GPC}_{\text{avg}}(\mathbf{u}) \propto \frac{1}{L}\int_0^L\theta(x,t_{\text{pulse}})\,dx
  \]
  and effective deposition rate:
  \[
  J_3(\mathbf{u}) = \frac{\text{GPC}_{\text{avg}}(\mathbf{u})}{t_{\text{cycle}}(\mathbf{u})}
  \]
  Maximize \(J_3\).

- **Constraints**:
  - Conformality:
    \[
    \frac{\theta(L,t_{\text{pulse}})}{\theta(0,t_{\text{pulse}})} \ge \eta_{\min}
    \]
  - Thermal budget bounds for \(T\), and mechanical/chemical windows for pressures.

- **Model usage**:
  - As above; now GPC is integrated over \(x\) and normalized by total cycle time.

### 4.4 Problem D – Process window screening for new precursor (chemistry choice)

Here some “material” parameters become design choices: \(M_A, c, P_0d_s\). This is more for **process/precursor development** than per‑run optimization.

- **Decision variables**:  
  \(\mathbf{v} = (M_A, c, P_0d_s)\) representing candidate precursors, plus recipe variables \((T,p_{A0},t_{\text{pulse}})\).

- **Objective**:
  Multi‑objective: maximize conformality and GPC, minimize required \(t_{\text{pulse}}\), subject to physical bounds on \(M_A, c, P_0d_s\).

- **Constraints**:
  - Chemistry feasibility (ranges of \(c\), stability at \(T\), etc.)
  - Same coverage/conformality constraints as above.

This uses the **same model**, but reclassifies some “material parameters” as design choices at **development** time.

## 5. Tunable vs non‑tunable parameters in normal ALD recipes

### 5.1 Tunable in a normal ALD recipe (run‑to‑run knobs)

Directly tunable (or at least strongly influenced):

- \(T\) – substrate / reactor temperature
- \(p_{A0}\) – precursor A partial pressure (via bubbler temperature, carrier flow, dose valve timing)
- \(p_B\) – carrier / background pressure to a limited extent
- \(t_{\text{pulse}}\) – precursor A pulse time
- Co‑reactant pulse time and pressure (affects other half‑cycle; not in this A‑side model)
- Purge times and flows (affect residual \(p_A\) and \(p_B\) between pulses, not explicitly modeled here)

Indirectly tunable (through system design, not recipe):

- \(H,W,L\) – feature geometry (fixed by the device design; not changed between wafers in normal operation)
- Reactor total pressure and flow patterns (within some range)

### 5.2 Not tunable per recipe (intrinsic)

Fixed by physics or chosen material/precursor system:

- Fundamental constants: \(N_0, R, k_B\).
- Intrinsic precursor properties:  
  \(M_A, d_A\), vapor density/boiling point (→ \(\rho\)), \(M_B,d_B\), \(\tau\).
- Surface kinetics parameters for a fixed chemistry:
  - \(c\) (sticking coefficient) – sometimes weakly temperature‑dependent but mainly chemical.
  - \(P_0 d_s\) – desorption prefactor.
  - Site density and film‑related factors in \(q\): \(b_A,b_{\text{film}},M,gP,C_{\text{sat}}\).
- Geometric dimensions of the device: \(H,W,L\) (unless you redesign the device).

### 5.3 Derived, but effectively fixed once system is chosen

Given the above fixed parameters, these are computed but not independently tunable:

- \(d_A\) from \(M_A,\rho,T\)
- \(v_A,z_A,D_A,D_{Kn},D_{\text{eff}}\) once \(T,p_B, H,W\) are chosen
- \(Q\), \(q\), \(K\) for a given \(T,p_A\) and chemistry
- \(p_A(x,t)\), \(g(x,t)\), \(\theta(x,t)\), \(x_s\) – they are the **model outputs** for a given recipe

## 6. Summary – Optimization mindset with this model

From an optimization perspective:

- **Design space** (fixed by integration & precursor choice):  
  \(H,W,L\), \(M_A, c, q, P_0d_s,\rho\) etc.

- **Control space / knobs**:  
  \(T,p_{A0},p_B,t_{\text{pulse}}\) (and co‑reactant conditions).

- **State space / outcomes**:  
  \(D_{\text{eff}}\), \(p_A(x,t)\), \(g(x,t)\), \(\theta(x,t)\), \(x_s\), and derived metrics (GPC(x), conformality, penetration).

The rate‑calculation chain is:

\[
\text{controls} \Rightarrow (D_{\text{eff}},p_A(x,t)) \Rightarrow g(x,t) \Rightarrow \theta(x,t) \Rightarrow \text{GPC}(x),\text{conformality},x_s
\]

and all reasonable optimization problems can be formulated as:

- choosing controls (and possibly chemistries)  
- to extremize objectives involving \(\theta(x,t_{\text{pulse}})\), \(x_s\), and GPC  
- subject to self‑limiting and process‑window constraints

with the key understanding that **material/geometry parameters are not knobs** in everyday ALD recipes, whereas pressures, temperature, and pulse times are.
